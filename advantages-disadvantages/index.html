Advantages and Disadvantages of CRISPR

Advantages

Efficient and customisable

CRISPR-Cas9 is a more efficient and customisable alternative compared to other existing genome editing tools.
No separate cleaving enzyme

CRISPR-Cas9 does not need to be paired with a separate cleaving enzymes.
Targets multiple genes

CRISPR-Cas9 can be used to target multiple genes simultaneously, instead of targeting genes one at a time, which most genome editing technologies can not do.
Developing models

CRISPR-Cas9 greatly reduces the time and expense of developing animal models that have specific genomic changes.

Disadvantages

Delivery of CRISPR

It is difficult to use CRISPR on a large number of cells, which is a common problem for most genome editing tools. Currently, viral vectors are the most common delivery method, but these do not always work well because of the host organism’s immune response.
Not 100% efficient or accurate

While it is rare, there may be unintended off-target effects, which can have severe consequences on the organism’s gene sequence.
Ethical issues

The possibility of editing the genome of germline cells and of embryos being used to produce ‘designer’ babies rather than curing birth defects or genetic diseases raises serious ethical issues.
