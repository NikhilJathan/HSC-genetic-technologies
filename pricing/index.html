Treating Genetic Diseases

It is possible to correct or replace faulty genes with functional ones through CRISPR. Furthermore, additional copies of functional genes may be introduced into an organism, helping to treat or even cure genetic diseases, such as cystic fibrosis with a known mutation.

Sickle Cell Disease

Through CRISPR, it is possible to treat sickle cell disease (SCD). CRISPR can be used to switch on functional copies of genes coding for haemoglobin, present in the detail haemoglobin genes (HbF).

Heart Disease

One-time treatments through CRISPR have the potential to permanently reduce the burden of heart disease on individuals suffering.

Development of Animal Models

Scientists can use CRISPR to more quickly and efficiently create genome models for animals and cells, which also accelerates research into diseases.

Rapid Diagnostic

With the further research and development of CRISPR, it is likely that it will be soon used as a rapid diagnostic, for various diseases.

Within Universities

$15 000-30 000 AUD

For research purposes within universities and laboroties, this is the standard cost of CRISPR procedures, however it is quite dependent on the nature of the work being done with CRISPR, and could be much more.

For Commerical/Medical Use

>$3 million AUD

In commercial medical use however, this price is much higher, and is usually over $3 million AUD for most medical treatments involving CRISPR, such as gene therapy.
